---
title: "Reconciliation"
description: "Scheduled reconciliation of scrubbing job statuses with Jobber."
---

The reconciliation module (`lib/scrubbing-reconciliation.ts`) periodically checks that scrubbing job statuses are consistent with their Jobber counterparts. This catches any updates missed by webhooks.

## Why reconciliation is needed

Webhooks can fail due to:
- Network issues between Jobber and MVSoftware
- Application downtime during webhook delivery
- Webhook retry exhaustion
- Race conditions during rapid status changes

Reconciliation acts as a safety net, ensuring data consistency even when webhooks are missed.

## How it works

The reconciliation cron job (`POST /api/cron/scrubbing-reconciliation`) runs every 15 minutes:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Fetch active jobs">
    Query all scrubbing jobs that are not in terminal statuses (`invoiced` or `archived`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Jobber status">
    For each active job, fetch the current status from the Jobber API.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compare and update">
    If the Jobber status has changed since the last check, update the scrubbing job and create a `system` comment recording the reconciliation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log results">
    Report the number of jobs checked, updated, and any errors to Sentry.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Conflict resolution

When reconciliation finds a discrepancy:
- If the Jobber status is ahead of the scrubbing status (e.g., Jobber shows complete but scrubbing shows in_field), the scrubbing status is updated to match
- If the scrubbing status is ahead (e.g., scrubbing shows ready_to_bill but Jobber shows active), the scrubbing status is preserved since it represents work done in MVSoftware
